## 📋 Overview

<a href="#familyTree">Family Tree</a> serves genealogy researchers documenting family history, family members who want to understand their ancestry, and anyone with historical family records and photographs who wants to organize them into a coherent family history.

The <a href="#familyTree">dedicated genealogy researcher use case</a> uses Family Tree as the primary tool for systematic family history research — working through ancestral lines methodically, sourcing each fact to documentary evidence, connecting with online databases for record discovery, and building a documented family history that will be accurate and useful for future generations. The <a href="#familyTree">casual family history use case</a> documents the family story for personal and family interest — recording grandparents' life stories, organizing family photographs into a tree structure, and creating a family history that current family members can access and future generations will value.

The <a href="#familyTree">family reunion and sharing use case</a> creates family tree documents and books to share at family gatherings — bringing disconnected branches of the family together through the shared visual representation of how everyone is related. <a href="#familyTree">DNA and genetic genealogy integration use case</a> uses Family Tree to make sense of DNA match results — linking DNA test results to the family tree to understand how DNA matches connect and to use DNA evidence in resolving research questions about family connections.</p>

<a href="#familyTree">Cloud sync for research continuity</a> synchronizes the family tree across multiple Macs — research continues on any device with the same tree available. <a href="#familyTree">Family sharing features</a> allow multiple family members to access and contribute to the shared family tree — collaborative genealogy where different family members contribute their knowledge and document finds.

<a href="#familyTree">Regular updates for database connections</a> maintain integration with FamilySearch, Ancestry, and other genealogy services as their APIs and record collections evolve. <a href="#familyTree">Synium Software development</a> — the makers of MacFamilyTree — bring years of Mac-specific genealogy application development to Family Tree with deep macOS integration.

## 📌 FAQ

<details>
<summary>What genealogy chart types does Family Tree produce?</summary>

Pedigree charts tracing direct ancestry, descendant charts showing all descendants, and other specialized genealogy visualization formats.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Family Tree store source citations for facts?</summary>

Yes. Documentary sources attach to life events — census records, birth certificates, and other evidence sources cited for each recorded fact.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Family Tree work for beginning genealogists?</summary>

Yes. The application accommodates both beginners building a first family tree and experienced researchers working with large documented trees.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I share my family tree from Family Tree?</summary>

Yes. Trees export in GEDCOM format for sharing, and family history books generate for sharing in book form.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What life events can I record for each person?</summary>

Birth, death, marriage, baptism, census, occupation, education, military service, immigration, and other standard genealogy event types.

</details>
